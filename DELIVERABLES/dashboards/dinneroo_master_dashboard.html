Data Sources & Methodology

Snowflake Behavioral Data

  • Repeat Rate: % customers with 2+ orders (n=57,320)
  • Average Rating: Mean star rating (n=9,759)
  • Order Volume: Total orders per zone

Survey Post-Order Survey

  • Kids Happy: % "Full and happy" (families only, n=461)
  • Liked/Loved: % "Loved it" or "Liked it" (n=955)
  • Enough Food: % Agree portions adequate (n=1,028)
  • Food Hot/On Time: Operational quality metrics
Why These Metrics? Dinneroo's success is defined by families returning and being satisfied. Repeat rate is our primary behavioral signal â€” it shows families found enough value to order again. Kids happy is the core family-specific outcome â€” if kids aren't satisfied, parents won't reorder. We triangulate behavioral data (what people do) with survey data (what people say) to validate thresholds from multiple angles.
Hierarchical Analysis: We analyzed factors in order of impact â€” partners first, then cuisines, then dishes per partner. This reveals the independent contribution of each factor to zone success.
