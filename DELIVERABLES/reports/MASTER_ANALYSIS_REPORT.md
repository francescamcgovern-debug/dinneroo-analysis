## 1. Executive Summary

### Key Strategic Principle

**Success = families returning happy, not just ordering once.**

Order volume is a survivorship bias metric - it shows activity, not success. We prioritize:
1. **Repeat Rate** (30% weight) - Are families coming back?
2. **Satisfaction/Rating** (25% weight) - Are families happy?
3. **Selection Adequacy** (20% weight) - Do we have the right cuisines/dishes?
4. **Volume** (10% weight) - LOWEST priority

### Core Positioning

**Dinneroo is designed for BALANCED MIDWEEK MEALS** - nutritious options families feel good about serving on a Tuesday.

- "Balanced" = protein + veg + carbs in reasonable proportions
- Grilled chicken with customizable sides is the ideal fit
- Not indulgent weekend treats

### Top 10 Actionable Recommendations

| Priority | Action | Evidence |
|----------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Recruit grilled chicken partners (Nando's-style) | 1,299 non-Dinneroo orders, 54% midweek, perfect positioning fit |

## 7. Data Sources and Methodology

### Data Sources

| Source | Sample Size | Use |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| **Snowflake Orders** | 80,724 | Order volume, repeat rate, ratings |
| **Snowflake Customers** | 50,744 | Customer-level metrics |

| **OG Survey** | 404 | Home cooking, wishlist, barriers |
| **Menu Catalog** | 63,990 | Dish availability |
| **Customer Transcripts** | 88 | Qualitative insights |
| **WBRs** | 10 | Partner execution context |
| **Full Order History** | 800,580 | Non-Dinneroo ordering patterns |

### Evidence Standards

| Level | Definition | Action |
|-------|------------|--------|
| **Unreliable** | n < 20 | Do not report |
| **Directional** | n = 20-50 | Report with caveat |
| **Moderate** | n = 51-100 | Report as finding |
| **Strong** | n > 100 | Report confidently |

### Key Methodological Notes

1. **Survivorship Bias**: Order volume shows what works among AVAILABLE options, not what families WANT. Always analyze latent demand via open-text.

2. **Dish Types, Not Brands**: Think about what families want to EAT, not specific partner names. "Grilled Chicken with Sides" not "Nando's".

3. **Menu Gap Analysis**: The same partner may serve DIFFERENT items on Dinneroo vs regular Deliveroo. Always check the menu catalog.

4. **Success Over Volume**: A zone with fewer orders but high retention is healthier than a zone with many orders but low retention.

### Limitations

- Survey data may not represent all family types
- Regional bias toward London/South East
- No explicit ethnicity data in surveys
- Some dish scores are estimated (not measured)
